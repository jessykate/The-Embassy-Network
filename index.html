Live & Collaborate in Locations Around the World

A Housing network for driven world changers


The Embassy Network is a private club for a new generation. It is a place to call home for professionals and creatives who travel the far reaches of the globe and the mind; a place to share a stimulating environment built around dynamic interaction, creativity, and intention where members can travel and always be at home.

As collaborative work spaces have changed the landscape of business, the Embassy Network pushes creativity to the next level - getting the right people in the room, outside of formality, to cultivate innovation like never before. Members have access to full-time and/or flexible-duration accommodations, and receive access to collaborative space, events, and a global community of like-minded individuals.

Co-Living

Each Embassy location will offer short and long term residential accommodations in a co-living, community setting. Long term residents live in the Embassy as their primary residence, provide an anchor to local culture, and are active participants in creating activities and events at their location.

Flexible Duration

In addition to long term accommodations, each Embassy provides short term accommodations operated much like a B&B. Members reserve space at any location in the network, anywhere around the world, as needed to support their travel, work, and collaborations.

Community Space

Every Embassy location includes a community space open to members and friends to gather and connect. These spaces differ per-location. From co-working space to restaurants, each community space is a key tie-in to local culture and a way to remain outward focused in ideation, problem-solving, and relationship-building.

Overview

Purpose-driven

The Embassy Network will be a coordinating body: connecting existing properties, coordinating membership, supporting new houses, and providing online resources.

Each individual property, or local embassy node, provides a curated space for organic and impromptu interactions. Each node has independent day-to-day operations and a distinct culture, hosting its own microcosm of workshops, salons, dinners, and other events. Residents are chosen by other residents, and the balance of rooms set aside for short- and long-term accommodation decided together between residents and the Global Embassy Network.

By leveraging co-location, the fresh perspectives that comes with travel, the organic interactions that happen in the home environment, and the power of food for building community, we feel that the investment that we and others make in ourselves can be vastly enhanced. Need a little time to focus and reflect? Don't worry, there's space to get away as well.

Locations

Travel and always be at home

The first Embassy nodes are targeted for San Francisco and DC: two urban centers with high concentrations of creative individuals, both resident and transient. We consider our "minimum viable product" to be at least two cities for testing the financial and membership model.

The ideal Embassy location has 3 specific functions:

  1. long-term residential rooms and/or suites,
  2. shorter-term accommodations,
  3. a public, possibly commercial, community-oriented space.

An archetypal design would have this community space on the ground floor, open to foot traffic, and supporting collaborations and human interactions, keeping a fresh flow of people and ideas through the space. Each location will decide the specific theme or purpose of this community space as a function of its environment, local needs and culture. It might be a co-working space, cafe, restaurant, bar, or something else. The second floor would house short-term accommodation rooms, as well as a large, industrial-grade, shared kitchen, common rooms, and library. Finally, the third floor would contain the longer term full time residences. These might be small suites, large rooms, or a combination of both.

Membership

Surround yourself with excellence.

Passion and drive are cultivated by a supportive external environment, and every Embassy location is a safe haven for new ideas, hard work, reflection, and fun. Membership in the Embassy Network is exclusive-- we want only those willing to assert, with no apologies, their passion for innovation, experimentation, and changing the world for the better. If you see yourself in these words, then you are home. There is no right or wrong. Only a shared purpose and committment.

Members may either be full-time residents at a specific location in the network, or use the flexible accommodations much like they would use a Bed and Breakfast: as a replacement for the anonymous hotels, mass-produced food, and cultural disconnect that so often comes with traveling for work.

Membership levels will exist to suit different needs, and include primary-residence, blocks of per-nite accomodation, and organizational membership.

Financial

A community-profit organization.

The Embassy Network is incorporated as a Benefit Corporation in the state of California. Our fundamental governing principle is to never profiteer off our community and our homes. Correspondingly, we have set out to design an organization that at its core is not-for-profit, but that may take investment if the community decides there is a valuable opportunity to scale. The founding board of directors have inserted a corporate bylaw stating that shareholders and directors agree not to take dividends from the corporation; that any future directors or shareholders must explicitly agree to this bylaw, and that changing this by law requires full consensus of all shareholders and directors. (thus any decision about taking investors would also require this consensus). This is an experiment is creating what we have been calling a "community-profit" organization.

Any future profits, should they exist, will be re-invested into Embassy Network activities, real estate purchases, or community funding to support projects of our members. Beyond this guiding principle and initial set of stipulations, we intend to select and experiment with decision making, fee and financial structures to ensure sustainability, together with members of the community.

The Embassy Network will partner with houses to evolve an operational and financial model. It will also offer services such as online booking and payment, house management software, insurance, permitting guidance, cultural, design and financial assistance getting new houses off the ground, and policy advisory/advocacy efforts.
